About

I am a Research Associate at IIT Kanpur working under Prof. Ashutosh Modi. My research explores fundamental questions about how language models learn and generalize: Why does a particular prediction follow? How can we understand the internal mechanisms that drive these decisions? And how can models adapt when the world changes?

These questions drive my work at the intersection of mechanistic interpretability, causal reasoning, and large language models. I believe genuine intelligence requires systems that learn underlying mechanisms, make interpretable decisions, and generalize without catastrophic forgetting.

The CIA Framework

My research is organized around three foundational principles:

Causality Understanding causal mechanisms beyond pattern matching
Interpretability Opening the black box to reveal computational primitives
Adaptability Enabling robust generalization and continual learning

My recent work on singular vector-based interpretability demonstrates that individual attention heads contain multiple independent computational primitives operating along orthogonal axes. This discovery suggests models learn a compact library of interpretable building blocks—computational atoms that combine into complex behaviors.
